Combat Ability

This determines your Intiative, your Accuracy and even Damage if you play it right.

Comabt Ability is equal to your Strength and your Dexterity.

Initiative

This determines when your turn is during battle and will change upon other battles.

Roll dice equal to your Combat Ability and compare with other party members to determine the order of operation.

Typical Play

During a player's turn they usually have a Simple Task and a Complex Task.

Simple Tasks include things like moveing and equip/dequip/requiping weapons.

Complex Tasks include things like attacking and using items.

Ask GM if you may perform certain actions you are unsure of.

Target Accuracy

Your target will have an unknown Target Accuracy, You must roll that number or over it to succesfuly land a hit.

Roll dice less than or equal to your Combat Ability.

If you don't roll the Target Accuracy or greater, your turn ends.

Damage

Check you weapon to see how many dice to roll.

If you didn't use all of your Combat Ability Dice, whatever is leftover maybe used to increase your damage.

The number rolled is the damage dealt to the enemy.

By your next turn you will have regenerated you Combat Ability.

Myr Attacks

Myr Attacks use Myr Ability Points rather than Combat Ability Points.

See Myr for more information on Myr.

Wild Die

.:Accuracy:.
1 - instant miss.
6 - instant hit.

.:Damage:.
1 - you take half your own damage rolled.
6 - roll again adding the previous roll to the new roll.
